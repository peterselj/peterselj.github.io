Why I write.

I don't chase clicks. My metric of choice is not "scale," or millions of viewers, or thousands of shares. My metric is dialogue.

I write to provoke intimate conversation among an engaged following, and to challenge myself.

So I don't keep a blog anymore (I did for about 10 years), but I write essays into Google Docs. This affords a unique semi-walled garden for individual access, which in turn empowers readers to express themselves more candidly than the public internet seems to afford. ("The internet never forgets," they say.) And the commenting ability (in-line for short form, via Gmail for lengthier prose) is better and easier than anything else I've seen.

In 2016, every 1,000 words I wrote turned into over 3,000 words in comments from 20 or fewer readers. That's my metric, and I'm proud of it.
